Recommended Problem: Longest Common Subpath (Hard)

Why This Problem is a Great Next Step

Building on your experience with problems like "House Robber II" and "Longest Common Prefix", tackling "Longest Common Subpath" will further enhance your algorithmic skills. Here’s why this problem is an excellent choice:

Key Similarities in Solving Techniques

This problem shares common ground with "Longest Common Prefix" as both involve finding commonality across multiple sequences. You will leverage similar pattern-matching techniques, but with a more complex data structure approach.

Algorithmic and Data Structure Skills Reinforced

This problem introduces and reinforces advanced concepts like:

  • Binary Search: Used here to efficiently determine the maximum length of the common subpath.
  • Rolling Hash: A technique to efficiently compare subpaths, building on your understanding of hash functions.
  • Suffix Array: A powerful data structure for solving substring problems, which complements your previous work with arrays and matrices.

Building on Previous Knowledge

Having solved "House Robber II", you've already encountered dynamic programming and optimization techniques. This problem takes those concepts further by requiring optimization of search space and efficient comparison of sequences.

New Patterns and Edge Cases

You'll learn to handle complex edge cases involving overlapping paths and varying path lengths, as well as optimizing hash collisions, which are crucial for ensuring the algorithm’s efficiency.

Conceptual and Difficulty Progression

Transitioning from medium to hard problems, "Longest Common Subpath" challenges your problem-solving and critical thinking skills. It serves as a valuable step in mastering advanced algorithmic strategies and prepares you for tackling even more complex challenges in competitive programming.

By solving this problem, you’ll not only solidify your understanding of arrays and hash functions but also gain exposure to new and sophisticated techniques that are essential for high-level algorithm design.
